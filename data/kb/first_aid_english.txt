Infectious Diseases
Q: What's diarrhea, and what should I do first?
A: Diarrhea is an intestinal infection from bacteria/viruses that messes up water
absorption in your gut, causing loose stools, cramps, and dehydration. It works by
inflaming the gut lining, forcing out fluids rapidly. If untreated, it leads to severe
weakness, organ failure, or even death from fluid loss. Natural first aid: Mix a
homemade ORS (1 liter water + 6 tsp sugar + ½ tsp salt + lemon juice), sip slowly, rest,
and skip heavy meals.
Q: Tell me about malaria.
A: Malaria comes from Plasmodium parasites spread by mosquitoes, infecting your red
blood cells and causing chills and cyclic fevers. The parasite multiplies in your liver then
bursts blood cells, releasing toxins. Untreated, it causes anemia, brain damage, or fatal
organ failure. Natural first aid: Brew tulsi or ginger tea to ease fever, stay super
hydrated, and use nets to block mosquitoes.
Q: How does dengue fever work?
A: It's a virus from Aedes mosquitoes causing high fever, killer joint pain, and rashes.
The virus damages blood vessels and drops platelet counts, leading to leaks. Without
treatment, you risk internal bleeding, shock, or death. Natural first aid: Drink coconut
water or fresh papaya leaf juice to boost hydration and platelets; rest in a cool spot.
Respiratory Issues
Q: Explain an asthma attack.
A: Asthma is chronic airway inflammation that flares with triggers like dust, narrowing
your breathing tubes. Immune chemicals swell the bronchi, blocking air. If ignored, it
can cause respiratory failure or lasting lung harm. Natural first aid: Breathe steam from
hot water with ginger slices, sit upright, and sip warm honey-lemon water.
Q: What's the deal with the flu (influenza)?
A: A respiratory virus that invades nose, throat, and lung cells, replicating fast to cause
fever, cough, and aches. Untreated, it can spark pneumonia, especially in kids or elderly.
Natural first aid: Make a ginger-garlic-tulsi decoction, drink lots of fluids, and get plenty
of rest.
Metabolic and Heat Issues
Q: What is hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)?
A: Common in diabetics, it's a sudden blood glucose crash from too much insulin or
missed meals, starving your brain and body. It triggers tremors and confusion.
Untreated: seizures, coma, or brain damage. Natural first aid: If conscious, give jaggery,
dates, or honey; then rest and monitor.
Q: How does heatstroke happen?
A: Your body overheats from heat or overexertion, failing to sweat and cool down, which
cooks your proteins. Symptoms: confusion, hot dry skin. If untreated: brain swelling,
organ shutdown, death. Natural first aid: Get to shade, sponge with cool water or ice
packs on neck/armpits, sip electrolyte drinks.
Allergic and Skin Problems
Q: Describe anaphylaxis.
A: A massive allergic overreaction to food/stings releases histamine, swelling your
throat and crashing blood pressure. It constricts airways fast. Untreated: airway block,
heart stoppage. Natural first aid: Try turmeric milk for inflammation; lie flat with legs
up if breathing's okay, seek help ASAP.
Q: What's a minor burn really?
A: Heat or chemicals damage skin proteins, sparking pain, redness, and blisters via
inflammation. Untreated: infection or scars. Natural first aid: Run cool water over it for
10-20 mins, then apply fresh aloe vera gel.
Q: Why do I get the common cold?
A: Viruses stick to nasal cells, inflaming your upper airways for runny nose and sore
throat. Untreated: it may lead to sinus or ear infections. Natural first aid: Sip
tulsi-ginger-lemon tea, do steam inhalation, use honey for soothing.
